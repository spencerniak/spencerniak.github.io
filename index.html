01 / Picking operations

Warehouse Operations Intelligence

Business context

A picking team had plenty of operational data but no shared view of where time and accuracy were leaking — across stores, categories, and shifts. Supervisors were reacting to problems a day after they'd already cost something.

Analytical approach

I modelled the data as a star schema — store, category, and date dimensions around a picking-events fact table — and wrote DAX measures for picking rate, session efficiency, and a replenishment priority index. The report is layered into three decision levels: operational for today's floor, tactical for this week's patterns, strategic for where to invest.

Insight & so what

A dynamic action banner names the single highest-priority bottleneck the moment the page loads, and a session quadrant scatter separates the fast-and-accurate pickers from the ones who need support. The supervisor stops reading charts and starts the shift already knowing the one thing to fix.

02 / Inbound performance

Inbound KPI Dashboard

Business context

Inbound performance was being judged on a fill-rate number that quietly blamed the picking team for problems that were really inventory shortfalls. The metric was eroding trust between teams and aiming fixes at the wrong place.

Analytical approach

I rebuilt the KPI definitions from first principles, separating picker error from inventory shortfall so each team owned the right number. That meant clean fill rate, perfect order rate, and units-short measures — plus narrative measures that write the headline in plain language.

Insight & so what

Once the two failure types were split apart, the real story surfaced: most "misses" were stockouts upstream, not mistakes on the floor. The conversation moved from finger-pointing to fixing replenishment — same data, finally pointed at the right cause.
